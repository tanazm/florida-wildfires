Florida combats wildfires that could threaten over 5 million housing units.


Florida has the third-largest number of housing units at risk of exposure to wildfires after California and Texas. But unlike California and Texas, Florida leads the nation in prescription burning — the act of setting planned fires to maintain the health of a forest and prevent wildfires.


Over 5 million housing units are at risk of wildfire in Florida, and 54 percent of those units are at risk of direct exposure, which can occur via contact with flames, radiant heat from burning wildland vegetation, or embers.


To help prevent wildfires, Florida engages in large scale prescription burning. The practice has helped Florida manage land, protect biodiversity, and prevent wildfires by ridding forests of dead leaves, shrubs, and other debris. In an average year, the Florida Forest Service issues about 88,000 prescription burning authorizations, allowing landowners and agencies to burn over 2.1 million acres.


Over 7 million housing units in California and 6.8 million units in Texas are at risk of exposure to wildfires, but the two states only burn an average of 125,000 and 400,000 acres each year, respectively.
