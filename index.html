Eight counties in Florida have the potential for high intensity wildfires.

Florida leads the nation in prescription burning, which is the act of setting planned fires to maintain the health of a forest. In an average year, the Florida Forest Service will issue about 88,000 authorizations, allowing landowners and agencies to burn over 2.1 million acres.

This practice has helped Florida manage land and protect biodiversity. It can also help prevent wildfires by ridding forests of dead leaves, shrubs, and other debris.

This is critical because there are eight counties in northern Florida that have more than a 50% probability of experiencing flame lengths greater than eight feet if a wildfire were to occur. According to The Wildfire Risk to Communities project, flame lengths greater than eight feet are beyond the limit of mechanical control and indicate the potential for high wildfire intensity.

The only state with more counties passing this threshold is Georgia with nine counties.
